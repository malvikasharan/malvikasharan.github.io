Malvika Sharan


I am a community builder, open science educator and facilitator of open source projects.
I am passionate about enabling collaborative culture, mentorship, accessibility and inclusive practices in data science.
Born and raised in India (Namaskar!), an EU national (Hallo!), based in London (Hi!).

About me


Community Manager - The Turing Way at The Alan Turing Institute
Co-founder - Open Life Science

As a community organiser and a recovering computational biologist, I am currently working on projects that aim to make computational and data science accessible and comprehensible for a wider community. I am always up for a chat about research in open science, best practices for open source and reproducibility, community building, collaboration, intersectionality, representation, technical training and computational biology.

After finishing my PhD in Bioinformatics at the University of WÃ¼rzburg in 2016, I joined European Molecular Biology Laboratory as a community coordinator. In 2019, I co-founded the Open Life Science mentoring and training program that teaches and mentors researchers from all around the world to design, lead and sustain Open Science projects. I am a fellow of the Software Sustainability Institute, and a board member of Open Bioinformatics Foundation, where I focus on training resources and fellowship programs to enhance the representation of marginalised groups in data science and research. Projects

Community manager, Research Associate

The Alan Turing Institute

The Turing Way is an open-source book project on best practices, guidance and recommendations on data science. I involve and support its diverse community of researchers, educators, policymakers and other stakeholders who develop and promote resources on reproducible, ethical, collaborative and inclusive data science.

September 2019 - Present

Co-Founder

Open Life Science

Open Life Science (OLS) is a non-profit organisation that provides an international platform for open science leaders and learners to exchange their knowledge. Through 16-week long personal mentorship and cohort-based training, our participants gain knowledge essential to create, lead, and sustain their projects openly. Listen to The ORION Open Science Podcast about OLS (Spotify link) to learn more.

June 2019 - Present

Board Member

Open Bioinformatics Foundation

Open Bioinformatics Foundation is a non-profit group that promotes open source software development and bioinformatics skills. In the board, I also chair the Event Fellowship Program that is aimed at financially supporting the participation of diverse members at events and communities promoting open source practices and skills within the biological research community.

August 2019 - Present

Fellow

Software Sustainability Institute

The Software Sustainability Institute (SSI) cultivates better, more sustainable, research software to enable world-class research. With my SSI fellowship, I focus on exploring and promoting effective open source practices that are suitable for low-income and low-tech research environment. It allows me to work on the intersection of community building, open source and inclusive approaches that enhance the representation of marginalised groups in STEM. Read more here.

2019 - Present

Trainer, Instructor and Chair of Code of Conduct Committee

The Carpentries

The Carpentries is a volunteer-based organisation that teaches foundational coding and data science skills to researchers worldwide. I am an instructor, instructor trainer and current chair of the Code of Conduct committee.

2015 - Present
